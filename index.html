AI and the blurring of insurance boundaries

The article says AI is creating liability exposures that do not fit cleanly into cyber, professional indemnity, or product liability policies, and that insurers are responding by revising wordings and building hybrid or standalone AI liability products. It explains that AI losses can arise from inaccurate advice, defective outputs, or automated decisions without a cyber event or conventional product defect, so insurers are adding endorsements and redefining terms such as professional services, technology products, and cyber events. This matters because broker and carrier coverage placement becomes uncertain, policy overlap and gaps increase, and organisations may need new AI-specific risk transfer options to preserve underwriting clarity and respond to claims efficiently.
