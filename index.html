AI ambition is exposing hidden cyber weaknesses in M&A deals

Aon’s head of transaction advisory services for EMEA, Ian McCaw, highlights that deploying agentic AI with elevated access to networks is reintroducing critical cyber vulnerabilities in M&A transactions, with risks transferring to new owners. Aon’s cyber diligence process evaluates exposed infrastructure, email security, and deep web intelligence to reveal hidden breaches and gaps in target companies’ cyber posture during transactions. This approach impacts deal valuations, warranty and indemnity insurance, and drives more cautious negotiation and underwriting practices, potentially reducing commercial broker margins and carrier exposure to undisclosed risks.

Agentic AI in Insurance Claims: Where to Start, What to Automate, and What to Keep Human-Controlled: By Vital Soupel

ScienceSoft published a detailed analysis on agentic AI deployment in property and casualty (P&C) insurance claims, highlighting examples such as Allianz’s Project Nemo and Travelers’ agentic voice service, with claims sectors adopting AI for intake, damage assessment, and fraud detection. Agentic AI coordinates multiple specialized AI tools into an automated workflow that can intake claims, verify coverage, assess damage, detect fraud, and escalate exceptions to humans, using layered controls and audit trails to meet regulatory requirements. The integration of agentic AI is reducing claims cycle time and loss-adjusting expenses, improving carrier underwriting efficiency and claims handling capacity while preserving human oversight for complex cases.

Space startups seek insurance for orbital AI data centers

Space startups backed by major players including SpaceX and Blue Origin are exploring insurance for proposed orbital AI data centers, as coverage will be essential to attract the financing needed to build these expensive space-based computing platforms. While insurers already cover traditional satellite risks such as launch failures, orbital debris, and space weather, they have little historical data to price the unique risks of AI hardware operating in space, making underwriting a challenge. Industry experts expect the market to remain small in the near term, but early discussions with brokers and insurers suggest the sector is beginning to take shape.
