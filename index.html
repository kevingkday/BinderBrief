How Digit is uing AI-led claims engine to speed up insurance settlements

Digit Insurance has implemented an AI-led, event-driven claims processing system that achieves near real-time settlements with specific performance metrics such as 60% of health insurance cashless hospitalizations approved within 20 minutes and 71% of motor repair approvals processed within 12 hours. The system automates document intake, classification, validation, and parallel decision-making steps using cloud-native technology and machine learning confidence models that automatically approve straightforward claims while routing complex cases to human reviewers. This approach improves operational efficiency, reduces manual workloads, enhances underwriting accuracy, and supports faster settlements that positively impact customer retention and claims leakage reduction.

Cyber, AI and economic pressures dominate insurance concerns in RiskScan 2026

RiskScan 2026 is a joint research study by the Insurance Information Institute (Triple-I) and Munich Re US based on a survey of more than 1,700 respondents, identifying cyber incidents, economic pressures, and artificial intelligence as the top insurance concerns. The study uses online survey data from U.S. and U.K. markets to analyze the convergence of cyber, economic, technological, and catastrophe risks and their systemic interconnections. These findings affect commercial underwriting by highlighting the need for updated cyber coverage, new catastrophe models, and AI-related product development, influencing insurer risk management and capacity allocation.

D&O Market Expected to Tighten Under Pressure, Says AM Best

Analysts at AM Best reported that the Directors & Officers (D&O) liability insurance market faced its fourth consecutive year of premium declines in 2025, with a direct loss ratio rising to 54.5% and reserve inadequacies noted for 2023 and 2024 accident years. The report highlights slower claims closure rates, increased underwriting risk due to geopolitical and regulatory factors, and the need for insurers to perform detailed due diligence on industry sectors while managing emerging risks such as AI disclosure and ESG compliance. These developments indicate pressure on underwriting margins, a tightening market capacity, and reduced broker flexibility in providing competitive D&O insurance products in 2026.

How broken interactions are costing insurers growth

Insurer Earnix identified a persistent gap between advanced back-office systems and customer-facing interactions despite widespread digital transformation investments over the past decade. This gap arises because key decision-making data—pricing logic, underwriting rules, and policy details—rarely integrates seamlessly into customer engagement channels, leading to delays and inconsistent responses during customer interactions. Closing this gap by embedding decisioning capabilities directly in customer workflows can improve underwriting efficiency, reduce operational costs, and enhance broker margins by reducing customer churn and improving retention.

New KYC Tool From Trustd -

Trustd has launched Know Your Carrier (KYC), a web-based tool providing instant risk assessments on carriers via email with inputs like email address and VAT number. The tool verifies email legitimacy, matches domain and business registration data, scans for adverse media, sanctions, court judgments, and uses Trustd’s network intelligence, integrating into existing workflows without installation. This tool aims to reduce cargo theft risks linked to carrier impersonation, improving underwriting accuracy and lowering loss exposure for commercial brokers and carriers in European freight markets.
